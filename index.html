Brazil

Brazil is known for its vibrant culture, diverse traditions, and stunning natural landscapes that range from the Amazon rainforest to the white-sand beaches of the Atlantic coast. Among its many landmarks, one of the most iconic is the Cristo Redentor (Christ the Redeemer) statue in Rio de Janeiro, a global symbol of both Christianity and Brazilian identity. Completed in 1931 after nearly a decade of planning and construction, the statue was designed by Brazilian engineer Heitor da Silva Costa and sculpted by French artist Paul Landowski, with Romanian sculptor Gheorghe Leonida contributing the detailed face. Standing 38 meters tall with arms stretching 28 meters wide, it is made of reinforced concrete and covered in soapstone tiles, chosen for their durability and smooth finish. Located at the summit of Corcovado Mountain in Tijuca National Park, the monument attracts millions of visitors each year who come to admire its grandeur and enjoy sweeping views of Rio, including Sugarloaf Mountain, Copacabana Beach, and Guanabara Bay.
